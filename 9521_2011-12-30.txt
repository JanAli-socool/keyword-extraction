<A NAME="Proposal_1"></A><P style="line-height:13pt; margin-top:0px; margin-bottom:14.667px; font-size:11pt" align=center><B>PROPOSAL 1
<BR>
<BR>
ELECTION OF TRUSTEES</B></P>
<P style="line-height:13pt; margin-top:0px; margin-bottom:14.667px; font-family:Times New Roman Bold; font-size:11pt"><B>Board Leadership
Structure</B></P>
<P style="line-height:13pt; margin-top:0px; margin-bottom:14.667px; padding-right:-2.4px; text-indent:48px; font-size:11pt">The primary
responsibility of the Board of Trustees (the Board) is to represent the interests of the shareholders of the Trust and to provide
oversight of the management of the Trust. &nbsp;The Trust&#146;s day-to-day operations are managed by Dinsmore Capital Management Co.
(Dinsmore Capital), the Trust&#146;s investment adviser. <B>&nbsp;</B></P>
<P style="line-height:13pt; margin-top:0px; margin-bottom:14.667px; text-indent:48px; font-size:11pt">The Board is divided into three
classes for purposes of election. &nbsp;One class is elected at each annual meeting of shareholders. &nbsp;Trustees in each class serve
for a three-year term. &nbsp;Classifying the Board for election may be regarded as an &#147;anti-takeover provision&#148; because it has
the effect of maintaining the continuity of the Board and requiring at least two years to change a majority of the Board.</P>
<P style="line-height:13pt; margin-top:0px; margin-bottom:14.667px; text-indent:48px; font-size:11pt">The Board currently consists of six
persons. &nbsp;Four of the trustees are &#147;independent,&#148; meaning they are not &#147;interested persons&#148; of the Trust within
the meaning of the Investment Company Act of 1940, as amended (the Investment Company Act). &nbsp;Two of the Trust&#146;s trustees are
&#147;interested persons&#148; because of their business and financial relationships with the Trust and Dinsmore Capital. &nbsp;The
Independent Trustees are represented by independent legal counsel at Board and committee meetings. &nbsp;Generally, each Board acts by
majority vote of all the Trustees, including a majority vote of the Independent Trustees if required by applicable law.</P>
<P style="line-height:13pt; margin-top:0px; margin-bottom:14.667px; text-indent:48px; font-size:11pt">The Board has established three
committees (Audit, Governance and Pricing, as described below). &nbsp;The Audit and Governance Committees are comprised solely of
Independent Trustees. &nbsp;The Board and its committees meet periodically throughout the year to oversee the Trust&#146;s activities,
review contractual arrangements with service providers, review the Trust&#146;s financial statements, oversee compliance with regulatory
requirements, and review pricing of fair value securities. &nbsp;The Board has determined that this leadership structure, including
having an Interested Trustee serve as chairperson, a supermajority of Independent Trustees serve on the Board, Audit and Governance
committee membership limited to Independent Trustees, and not having a lead independent trustee is appropriate because it </P>

<P style="margin:0px">&nbsp;</P>
<P STYLE="line-height:13pt; margin:0px; padding-left:0px; font-size:11pt" ALIGN="CENTER">2</P>

<P style="margin:0px">&nbsp;</P>
<HR SIZE=5 COLOR=GRAY NOSHADE>
<PAGE>
<P style="margin:0px">&nbsp;</P>

<P style="line-height:13pt; margin-top:0px; margin-bottom:14.667px; text-indent:0px; font-size:11pt">allows the Board to exercise
informed and independent judgment over the matters under its purview, and it allocates areas of responsibility among committees of
Trustees and the full Board in a manner that enhances effective oversight.</P>
<P style="line-height:13pt; margin-top:0px; margin-bottom:14.667px; font-size:11pt"><B>Board&#146;s Role in Risk Oversight</B></P>
<P style="line-height:13pt; margin-top:0px; margin-bottom:14.667px; text-indent:48px; font-size:11pt">The day-to-day management of
various risks relating to the administration and operation of the Trust is the responsibility of the Trust&#146;s investment adviser,
Dinsmore Capital, and other service providers retained by the Trust. &nbsp;The Board oversees this risk management function consistent
with and as part of its oversight duties. &nbsp;The Board performs this risk management oversight function directly and, with respect to
various matters, through committees established by the Board. &nbsp;Each committee reports its activities to the Board on a regular
basis. &nbsp;The following description provides an overview of many, but not all, aspects of the Board&#146;s oversight of risk
management for the Trust. &nbsp;The Board does not believe it is practicable to identify all of the risks that may impact the Trust or to
develop procedures or controls that are designed to eliminate all such risk exposures and that applicable securities law regulations do
not contemplate that all such risks be identified and addressed.</P>
<P style="line-height:13pt; margin-top:0px; margin-bottom:14.667px; text-indent:48px; font-size:11pt">The Board, working with personnel
of Dinsmore Capital and other service providers, has endeavored to identify the primary risks that confront the Trust. &nbsp;In general,
these risks include, among others, investment risks, credit risks, liquidity risks, valuation risks, operational risks, reputational
risks, regulatory risks, risks related to potential legislative changes and the risk of conflicts of interest affecting affiliates of
Dinsmore Capital in managing the Trust. &nbsp;The Board has adopted and periodically reviews various policies and procedures that are
designed to address these and other risks confronting the Trust. &nbsp;In addition, the officers of the Trust, the Trust&#146;s
investment adviser and other service providers to the Trust have implemented a variety of processes, procedures and controls designed to
address particular risks to the Trust. &nbsp;The Board and persons retained to render advice and service to the Board periodically review
and/or monitor changes to and developments relating to the effectiveness of these policies and procedures.</P>
<P style="line-height:13pt; margin-top:0px; margin-bottom:14.667px; text-indent:48px; font-size:11pt">The Board requires officers of the
Trust to report to the full Board on a variety of matters at regular and special meetings of the Board and its committees, as applicable,
including matters relating to risk management. &nbsp;The Audit Committee also receives reports from the Trust&#146;s independent
registered public accounting firm on internal control and financial reporting matters. &nbsp;The Board meets with the Trust&#146;s Chief
Compliance Officer periodically to discuss compliance matters and, on a quarterly basis, receives a report from the Chief Compliance
Officer regarding compliance matters. &nbsp;The Independent Trustees meet in executive session with the Trust&#146;s Chief Compliance
Officer at least annually. &nbsp;The Board, with the assistance of the Trust&#146;s management, reviews investment policies and risks in
connection with its review of the Trust&#146;s performance. &nbsp;In addition, the Board receives reports from Dinsmore Capital on the
investments and securities trading of the Trust. &nbsp;With respect to valuation, the Board has approved fair valuation procedures
applicable to valuing the Trust&#146;s securities, which the Board and the Audit Committee periodically review. &nbsp;The Board also
requires Dinsmore Capital to report to the Board on other matters relating to risk management on a regular and as-needed basis.</P>

<P style="line-height:13pt; margin-top:0px; margin-bottom:14.667px; padding-right:-2.4px; text-indent:48px; font-size:11pt">The
Governance Committee is comprised entirely of independent trustees (Dr.&nbsp;Bogan and Mr.&nbsp;Platt, serving as Chairman). &nbsp;In
addition, all such members are independent as such term is defined by the NYSE Amex Company Guide.</P>
<P style="line-height:13pt; margin-top:0px; margin-bottom:14.667px; padding-right:-2.4px; text-indent:48px; font-size:11pt">In accordance
with its charter, the Committee, among other duties, is responsible for: &nbsp;(i) nominating persons for election or appointment:
&nbsp;(a)&nbsp;as additions to the Board; (b) to fill vacancies which, from time to time, may occur on the Board; and (c) for election by
shareholders of the Trust at meetings called for the election of trustees; (ii) nominating persons for appointment as members of each
committee of the Board, including, without limitation, the Audit Committee, the Governance Committee, and the Pricing Committee; (iii)
reviewing from time to time the compensation, if any, payable to the trustees and making recommendations to the Board regarding
compensation; (iv) reviewing and evaluating from time to time the functioning of the Board and the various committees of the Board; (v)
overseeing the selection of independent legal counsel to the independent trustees;
and (vi) monitoring the performance of independent legal counsel employed by the Trust and the independent trustees.</P>
<A NAME="OLE_LINK7"></A><A NAME="OLE_LINK8"></A>
<P style="line-height:13pt; margin-top:0px; margin-bottom:14.667px; padding-right:-2.4px; text-indent:48px; font-size:11pt">A current
copy of the Governance Committee&#146;s Charter is available at the Trust&#146;s website at www.bancroftfund.com/BCVcorp.htm.</P>
<P style="line-height:13pt; margin-top:0px; margin-bottom:14.667px; text-indent:48px; font-size:11pt">Prior to a meeting of the
shareholders of the Trust called for the purpose of electing trustees, the Governance Committee will nominate one or more persons for
election as trustees at such meeting. &nbsp;The Governance Committee is also responsible for nominating trustees to fill vacancies
resulting from an increase in the size of the Board or as a result of the resignation, death or removal of a trustee. &nbsp;The
independent trustees are generally authorized to elect nominees to fill such vacancies.</P>
<P style="line-height:13pt; margin-top:0px; margin-bottom:14.667px; text-indent:48px; font-size:11pt">Evaluation by the Governance
Committee of a person as a potential nominee to serve as a trustee, including a person nominated by a shareholder, should result in the
following findings by the Governance Committee: &nbsp;(i) with respect to the nomination of independent trustees only, upon advice of
independent legal counsel to the independent trustees, that the person will qualify as an independent trustee, and that the person is
otherwise not disqualified under the Investment Company Act or the rules of the NYSE Amex Equities stock exchange from serving as a
trustee of the Trust; (ii)&nbsp;with respect to the nomination of independent trustees only, that the person is free of any material
relationship with the Trust (other than as a shareholder of the Trust), that would interfere with the exercise of independent judgment;
(iii)&nbsp;that the person is willing to serve, and willing and able to commit the time necessary for the performance of the duties of a
trustee; (iv)&nbsp;that the person can make a positive contribution to the Board and the Trust, </P>

the person&#146;s education, business experience, and such other factors as the Governance Committee may consider relevant; (v)&nbsp;that
the person is of good character and high integrity; (vi)&nbsp;that the person has desirable personality traits including independence,

experience, viewpoints, occupation, gender and race when considering potential nominees. </P>
<P style="line-height:13pt; margin-top:0px; margin-bottom:14.667px; text-indent:48px; font-size:11pt">Consistent with the Investment

<P style="line-height:13pt; margin-top:0px; margin-bottom:14.667px; text-indent:48px; font-size:11pt">The Governance Committee will
consider nominees recommended by a shareholder to serve as trustee, provided that: &nbsp;(i) such nominating person is a shareholder of
record at the time he or she submits the name of such nominee, (ii) such person is a shareholder of record at the time of the meeting at
which shareholders are elected, (iii) such shareholder is entitled to vote at the meeting of shareholders at which trustees will be
elected; and (iv) the Governance Committee shall make the final determination of persons to be nominated. &nbsp;The Governance Committee
will evaluate nominees recommended by a shareholder to serve as trustees in the same manner as they evaluate nominees identified by the
Governance Committee.</P>
<P style="line-height:13pt; margin-top:0px; margin-bottom:14.667px; padding-right:-2.4px; text-indent:48px; font-size:11pt">A shareholder

&nbsp;(i)&nbsp;is a shareholder of record at the time of giving notice to the Trust as described in (iv) below; (ii) is a shareholder of
record at the time of the 2013 Annual Meeting, (iii)&nbsp; is entitled to vote at the 2013 Annual Meeting; and (iv)&nbsp;has complied
with the notice procedures in the Trust&#146;s bylaws. &nbsp;Such notice procedures require that a shareholder submit the nomination in
writing to the Secretary of the Trust no earlier than October&nbsp;19, 2012 but no later than November&nbsp;19, 2012. &nbsp;The notice
must contain all information relating to the nominee required for proxy solicitations by Regulation 14A under the Exchange Act (including
the individual&#146;s written consent to being named in the proxy statement as a nominee and to serving as a trustee if elected).
&nbsp;The notice must also contain the shareholder&#146;s name and address as they appear on the Trust&#146;s books (and the name and
address of any beneficial owner, on whose behalf the nomination is made) and the number of shares of beneficial interest owned
beneficially and of record by such shareholder and beneficial owner.</P>

<P style="line-height:13pt; margin-top:0px; margin-bottom:14.667px; text-indent:48px; font-size:11pt">The Trust adopted Shareholder

Board. &nbsp;If a shareholder sends a recommendation of a nominee to the Board or to an individual trustee, such communication would be
covered by the Procedures. &nbsp;Shareholder proposals submitted pursuant to Rule 14a-8 under the Exchange Act, and communications made
in connection with such proposals, are not subject to the Procedures. &nbsp;The Trust&#146;s bylaws also contain provisions requiring a
shareholder to provide advance notice of his or her intention to nominate, at the Trust&#146;s annual meeting of shareholders, an
individual for election as trustee.</P>

regular mail or delivery service to the following address: &nbsp;Bancroft Fund Ltd., 65 Madison Avenue, Suite 550, Morristown, NJ 07960.
&nbsp;Email communications may be sent to: info@bancroftfund.com. &nbsp;All shareholder communications that are directed to the Board or
an individual trustee of the Trust in his or her capacity as trustee and received by the Shareholder Relations Group shall be promptly
forwarded to the individual trustee of the Trust to whom they were addressed or to the full Board, as applicable. &nbsp;Copies of all
such shareholder communications will also be distributed to the Chairs of the Trust&#146;s Audit Committee and Governance Committee, and
to counsel for the Trust and for the independent trustees. &nbsp;Counsel for the Trust and for the independent trustees, upon receipt of
its copy of a shareholder communication, shall work with such Chairs and counsel for the independent trustees to determine whether such
shareholder communication should be distributed to any trustees to whom it was not sent and whether and in what manner the trustees
should respond to such shareholder communication. &nbsp;Responses, if any, to shareholder communications shall be coordinated by counsel
for the Trust and for the independent trustees, working with the Chairs.</P>
